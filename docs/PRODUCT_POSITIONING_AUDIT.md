# Product Positioning Audit

Scope: every user-facing route, reusable component, and repo-stored
metadata surface, reviewed for (a) remaining alcohol/drinking association
and (b) whether the product reads as a distinct, valuable "party dice"
game rather than a bare random-prompt generator. This audit was done
*after* the four confirmed alcohol-content removals in Phase 1 Part A, to
check whether removal alone is sufficient — per the correction that Apple
reviews the whole submitted experience, not four isolated strings.

There is no PWA manifest, `index.html`, or separate onboarding screen in
this repo — the HTML shell is generated by `src/routes/__root.tsx`, and the
app redirects straight from `/` into `/app/classic` with no first-run
explanation screen. That absence is itself a finding (see Q1/Q5 below).

## Answers to the required questions

**1. What is the app's value proposition on first launch?**
There isn't an explicit one. `/` and `/app/` both redirect straight into
Classic — a bare "pick 1–6 dice, tap or shake, see the total" roller. No
copy anywhere on that screen explains that Caroline is a customizable
party-prompt platform. A first-time user (or reviewer) has to navigate to
the Custom tab themselves to discover the actual differentiator.

**2. Could a reviewer still reasonably interpret it as a drinking game?**
Not from anything currently shipped in this repo — the four explicit
alcohol references are gone, and the positioning-table sweep above found
no other confirmed alcohol term or imagery in code, config, or legal
pages. The remaining risk is entirely outside this repository: App Store
screenshots, subtitle, description, and keywords in App Store Connect are
not stored here and could not be inspected or changed as part of this
audit (see `docs/PHASE_1_RESULT.md`).

**3. Are Classic, Party, and Pro meaningfully different?**
Not really, mechanically. Classic and pack-rolling (`/pack/$id`,
`/party/$code`) are the same "roll → see a result" interaction with
different content sources (numeric dice vs. a pack's six sides). Party is
a join/share mechanism for packs made in Custom, not an independent game
mode. Pro is a paywall. Functionally there are two distinct interactions
(freeform dice, content-pack dice) plus a sharing feature and a purchase
screen — not four differentiated experiences.

**4. Does the app offer value beyond displaying random dice prompts?**
Limited today. The real value-add is user-authored content (text/emoji/
photo per side, with automatic background-removal for photo cutouts) and
time-boxed, no-account link sharing. There's no session/turn structure,
scoring, or multi-step game flow anywhere — every mode reduces to
"press Roll, see one card."

**5. Is Custom Dice sufficiently prominent to count as a differentiating capability?**
No, not as currently placed. It's the third of four bottom-nav tabs, and
creating a pack is Pro-gated — a free user's first exposure to it is an
upsell card, not the capability itself. Given it's the strongest
differentiator the app has, it's currently undersold.

**6. Which existing capabilities help distinguish the app without requiring a complete rebuild?**
Custom Dice authoring (text/emoji/photo sides, cutout processing), Party
Link sharing (time-boxed, no backend/account), theming (5 visual themes),
adjustable dice size/sound, and shake-to-roll. All are real, working, and
reusable — a stronger first-run framing and better placement of Custom
Dice could improve defensibility without new architecture.

**7. What minimum Phase 2 changes would most improve its App Store defensibility?**
- Add a brief first-launch framing (even a single explanatory line or
  one-time card on the Classic screen) naming the product as a
  customizable party-game dice app and pointing at Custom Dice.
- Move Custom Dice higher in perceived priority (nav order or a surfaced
  entry point from Classic) since it's the actual differentiator.
- Resolve the two ambiguous "Penalty Chain" / "King's Order" entries in
  `preset_party` with unambiguous wording so no reviewer interpretation
  question remains at all.
- Add one or two more non-alcohol preset packs so "party dice" reads as a
  broad, general-purpose category rather than a single repurposed preset.
- Outside this repo: verify and replace App Store screenshots, subtitle,
  keywords, and description if any reference drinking — this cannot be
  confirmed or fixed from source, see `docs/PHASE_1_RESULT.md`.
